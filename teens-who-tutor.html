Who We Are

About Us

Teens Who Tutor is a student-run non-profit tutoring initiative dedicated to helping underprivileged kids in New York City and across the nation gain access to quality academic support — completely free of charge.

Our tutors are 15–16 year old high school students at Trinity School in New York City. We believe that peer-to-peer learning creates a unique bond — students relate to us because we're close in age and still remember what it's like to learn these subjects for the first time.

All sessions are conducted online via Zoom, making it easy for families anywhere in the country to participate. Each session is 30 minutes and tailored to the student's specific needs.

Why We Do This

Our Mission

At Teens Who Tutor, we believe that every child deserves access to quality academic support regardless of their background or zip code. As a fully non-profit initiative, we charge nothing for our sessions — because learning shouldn't come with a price tag.

We are a team of 15–16 year old students who know firsthand what it's like to struggle with a subject, discover a passion for it, and ultimately master it. That shared experience is the heart of peer learning — and it's what makes our sessions different.

Our goal is simple: to give every student in grades K–8, whether in New York City or across the country, the personalized attention and encouragement they need to thrive. One session at a time, we're working toward a more equitable future in education.
